Hierarchy of Terms

A structured framework that organizes terms related to threat intelligence from general to specific.

Description

In the context of data sources for threat intelligence, the 'Hierarchy of Terms' refers to a systematic arrangement of concepts and terminology that helps analysts categorize and interpret data effectively. This hierarchy typically begins with broad categories that encompass various threats and gradually narrows down to specific types of threats or indicators. For instance, at the top level, one might have categories like 'Cyber Threats', which could further branch into 'Malware', 'Phishing', and 'Insider Threats'. Each of these categories can then be subdivided into more specific terms, such as 'Ransomware' under 'Malware' or 'Spear Phishing' under 'Phishing'. This hierarchical organization is crucial for helping security professionals prioritize their responses, understand relationships between different threat types, and facilitate effective communication within teams and across organizations. By using a well-defined hierarchy of terms, threat intelligence teams can create a common language that enhances collaboration and improves situational awareness.

Examples

  • General Category: Cyber Threats → Specific Type: Malware → Specific Example: Ransomware
  • General Category: Physical Threats → Specific Type: Insider Threats → Specific Example: Data Theft by Employee

Additional Information

  • A well-defined hierarchy aids in efficient data retrieval and analysis.
  • Hierarchies can vary between organizations and may evolve as new threats emerge.
