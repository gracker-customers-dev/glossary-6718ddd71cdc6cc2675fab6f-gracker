Tagging

The process of assigning descriptive labels or keywords to data for better categorization and retrieval.

Description

In the context of threat intelligence, tagging refers to the practice of applying labels to various data sources, such as threat reports, indicators of compromise (IOCs), vulnerabilities, and other relevant information. This process helps analysts quickly identify and categorize threats based on different criteria, such as severity, type, or source. By tagging data effectively, organizations can streamline their threat intelligence workflows, making it easier to filter, sort, and search through large volumes of information. For instance, tags can denote the nature of an attack, the affected systems, or the tactics, techniques, and procedures (TTPs) employed by adversaries. Furthermore, tagging can facilitate collaboration among teams by providing a shared vocabulary and context for discussing threats. Overall, tagging enhances situational awareness and enables more efficient incident response and threat mitigation strategies.

Examples

  • Tagging a malware sample with labels such as 'ransomware', 'Windows', and 'C2' to indicate its characteristics and behavior.
  • Applying tags to threat reports based on the attacker group involved, such as 'APT28' or 'Lazarus Group', to facilitate quick reference.

Additional Information

  • Tags can be hierarchical, allowing for more granular categorization (e.g., 'malware' -> 'ransomware' -> 'AES-Ni ransomware').
  • Tagging can be automated using machine learning algorithms to enhance the efficiency of threat intelligence processes.
